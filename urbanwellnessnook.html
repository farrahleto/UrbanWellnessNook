Fact #1: In low-income areas, life expectancy can be significantly lower compared to wealthier regions. On average, there might be a difference of several years in life expectancy between high and low-income neighborhoods within the same city or region.


Fact #2: In urban areas, disparities in healthcare access might be more pronounced due to higher population density. This density can exacerbate issues related to healthcare resource allocation, leading to a strain on existing healthcare services.


Fact #3: Urban areas tend to be hubs for healthcare innovation and technology. However, access to these advancements might be limited in low-income neighborhoods due to cost barriers or lack of awareness.
